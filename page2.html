CHEYENNE - The weather may have been miserable Friday, but that didn't keep Cheyenne residents from making the trip to Lions Park to make a donation to those in need.

In fact, traffic stopping by the Cheyenne-Kiwanis Community House for the 10th annual Day of Giving was as steady as the rainfall, with a regular stream of vehicles pulling up throughout the day to unload bags of food, craft supplies and personal hygiene items.

"It's been going great. (Thursday) night, we had our youth event, and kids were in here working like crazy," said Day of Giving President Caroline Veit. "They collected more than 8,000 pounds of food; that's double what they raised last year. If we grow exponentially like that, can you imagine how much help we're going to give those who are sick or in need?"

CHEYENNE - A Washington, D.C.-based lobbyist said he is hopeful that F.E. Warren Air Force Base and the Wyoming National Guard can avoid major budget cuts in the near future.

Automatic across-the-board defense spending reductions, known as sequestration, could be triggered later this year unless Republicans and Democrats in Congress can reach a budget deal.

But J.R. Reskovac with Capitol Decisions Inc. told local business leaders Friday that Congress is "under pressure" to make an agreement.

"The real fulcrum is going to come in September when once again we have to vote to raise the national debt ceiling," he said. "And with that, we are hoping that there will be either a short- or long-term budget agreement ... that would lead to some long-term or short-term certainty for the Department of Defense."
